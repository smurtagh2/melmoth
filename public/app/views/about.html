Topic Model of 60 Irish novels published between 1800 and 1830

Below is a visualisation of a topic model generated by MALLET. It divides the novels based on language used and by the subjects the authors were writing about. Hovering over a node will reveal the title of that novel. At the centre of each cluster of novels, delineated by colour, is a node which details the common topic of those novels. The large, grey chunk of novels is too broad to be assigned a single topic and is therefore classed as the "centre" of Irish novel-writing in this period. The other five clusters are the outliers of this period, and are dissimilar from the majority in the grey cluster. These include Gothic novels (Melmoth the Wanderer forms part of this cluster, unsurprisingly), novels concerned with Time and Place, novels explicitly about Irish issues, heavily romantic novels, and novels about Family and the Home.
